Neurological Diseases

For many years, a handful of heritable disorders puzzled geneticists by showing a tendency of the disease phenotype to become more severe or have earlier age-of-onset as the disease is passed on through subsequent generations in a family. This has been termed genetic anticipation, which is similar to the Sherman paradox described in fragile X syndrome, where the likelihood of having an affected child increases through subsequent generations of a pedigree. n. In contrast, even moderate expansions are sufficient to cause disease when they occur in the coding regions of genes as seen with the CAG repeats in the spinocerebellar ataxias. In a coding region, CAG codes for glutamine (Q), so CAG repeats result in a polyglutamine tract. These diseases are commonly referred to as polyglutamine (or polyQ) diseases.

Huntington disease

Huntington's disease is a rare, inherited disease that causes the progressive breakdown (degeneration) of nerve cells in the brain. Huntington's disease has a wide impact on a person's functional abilities and usually results in movement, thinking (cognitive) and psychiatric disorders.

Ataxia

Ataxia is a term for a group of disorders that affect co-ordination, balance and speech. Any part of the body can be affected, but people with ataxia often have difficulties with: balance and walking. speaking.
